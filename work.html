Food Ordering Mobile App

Street Vendor

This food ordering app focuses on a gap in the market, street food. And by street food, I don't mean the trending food carts and truck pods concentrated in various major cities. I'm talking about, the entrepreneurial immigrant vendors that see a need and set up shop on the busy streets of every major city in the U.S. There is such a rich culture in these street vendor communities and the Street Vendor app aims to offer an insight into these culinary cultural experiences. Anyone, from a self proclaimed foodie, a busy worker looking for a quick bite, to a native of that city will be able to locate local food vendors in their area without having to rely on word of mouth or sheer luck.

Mental Health Mobile App + Responsive Website

Feel

This project focuses on the design of a mental health app, aimed at addressing the needs of poc youth. To accompany the app a responsive website was also designed with a focus on providing information, to patrons, about the nonprofit company and the product(app).

The Feel app's purpose is to fill a vital gap in the mental health services available to young people of color. Due to barriers like racial disparities, access to mental health services is an obstacle faced by marginalized youth. The Feel app aims to address this issue by offering a convenient way for poc youth to access to mental health services for help with many mental health concerns like stress, anxiety, and depression.  

Responsive Website

ACID Skate Shop

The purpose of ACID skate shop's responsive website is to provide shoppers with a streamlined process for building custom skateboards online. The website aims to provide a simple and convenient online skateboard shopping experience while also, fostering diversity and inclusion by focusing on showcasing professional skateboarders of diverse backgrounds. Ultimately, the project aims to create an intuitive design equipped with full skateboard customization features.
