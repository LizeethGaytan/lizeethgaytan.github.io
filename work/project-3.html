The Product
The purpose behind the Feel app is to fill a vital gap, the racial and ethnic disparities in youth mental health, in the mental health field. With access to mental health care being a primary obstacle faced by young people of color, Feel app will offer an avenue for poc youth to reach out to for mental health concerns like stress anxiety and depression.
The Problem
Poc youth faces significant barriers for mental health resources due to racial disparities and there are no free mental health apps that focus on the experience of poc youth.
The Goal
Help teenagers take steps towards improving their mental health by providing free access to mental health services and resources.


Research

Summary

The research conducted for this project is split into two sections one for the product and another for the user. For instance, the product research is composed of a competitive audit, which informed market gaps. While the user research’s interview based  quantitative data, informed the development of the initial user pain points, the personas with their respective problem statements, the user flow, and the journey map. Ultimately the goal of research is to empathize with users and define product problems.

Product

Without a tangible product, but with access to numerous direct competitor apps, conducting a competitive audit as part of the product research was the best route. The competitive audit analysis demonstrated two major opportunities, one is that the mental health/wellness market lacks inclusivity. While the second opportunity is that none of the mental health apps in the market feature content and resources tailored to poc youth.

Competitive Audit

The competitive audit takes into account three direct and one indirect competitor. Three of these competitors are medium sized companies and only one is a large company. By quickly glancing at the competitive audit below you'll have a more in depth understanding of each competitor.

User

The user research is composed of quantitative user demographics and qualitative user experience research. In order to gain general knowledge about the target audience, straightforward research on user demographics was conducted. While user interviews were the most fitting research method for the user centered design approach I like to take.

User demographics research confirmed initial assumptions that primary users face reduced access to mental health services due to numerous disparities; alongside, the fact that 98% of them own a smartphone and are between the ages of 13 - 19. The research also revealed that users in this age group seek to de-stigmatize mental health services. By fostering an environment of inclusion through providing an app specifically created with the needs of poc youth in mind,  focusing on unique identities by providing tailored search options, and offering various online mental health resources to create an app that meets the needs of the specific user population, poc youth.

Design and Evaluation

In this section of the case study I'll be going over the steps I took to design the mental health app and responsive website. The design process begins with paper wireframes, a low risk brainstorming tool used to speed up the design workflow, I view them as an early information architecture planning aid. While the remaining steps are a cyclical iteration of perfecting digital wireframes and improving prototypes through the implementation of usability studies. Ultimately, the purpose of following through with the design process is to create a functional user friendly design. The end goal isn't perfection, but rather being aware that there is always room for improvement.

Usability Study: App

The primary goals of early product testing ,usability study, is to discover issues early in the design process is a cost effective practice. Regarding the study, it's unmoderated, composed of six participants, and broken down into the following parts: a usability research study plan, the usability notes, an affinity diagram, the findings, and a brief explanation on the design iteration that resulted from the study.

Usability Research Study Plan

The research study plan serves as an overview of the study and is a great tool to refer back to while conducting the usability study. Overall, it's a great reference point when conducting the study.

Usability Study Notes

The notes for the usability study are organized into five sections, the most important of which focus on a task completion rate of 1-3 and comments made by the user.

Usability Study Notes link
Affinity Diagram

The affinity diagram analyzes and categorizes the observations collected from the usability study notes. It's a precursor to building insights on the observations and groups recurrent information.

Findings

Findings from the usability study focused on guiding the design from low fidelity prototype to mockup.

Design Iteration

Iterating on the design is where insights identified, through the analysis of the affinity diagram, are implemented to ameliorate user pain points. The changes made to the app design include: making sure all search/filtering features are consistent through the app, removing a carousel which was detracting navigation ease, and adding interactive visual indicators to buttons throughout the design.

Usability Study: Website

The usability study for the website has a different structure due to the fact that the user needs differ from the app. For instance, its focus is on users interested in learning about the company. The study includes a research plan, usability study notes, and the findings.

Usability Study Research Plan

The usability study research plan is an outline that breaks down the study into digestible sections. Referencing back to particular sections while moving through the study is easier, in my opinion, due to the plans structure.

Usability Study Notes

Instead of starting with an affinity diagram I decided to categorize observations , made during the usability study, and possible pain points into a table. The accumulation of points on observations are what informed the user pain points.
