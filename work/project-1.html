The Product
The purpose of ACID skate shop's responsive website is to provide shoppers with a streamlined process for building custom skateboards online. The website aims to provide a simple and convenient online skateboard shopping experience, while fostering diversity and inclusion by focusing on showcasing professional skateboarders of diverse backgrounds.
‍
The Problem
There are two major problems that that ACID skate shop, with the assistance of an adequate website, proposes to solve and they focus on DEI( diversity, equity, inclusion) and a gap in the market. Focusing on the latter, there is a lack of online skateboard shops that offer full skateboard customization services. Now shedding light on a broader social issue, people from marginalized groups are underrepresented within the skateboard industry.
‍
The Goal
The primary market goal is to ensure ACID skate shop has a competitive edge by delivering a website with features that allow customers to efficiently build a fully customized skateboard.

The social impact goal is to ensure that regardless of gender, race, sexual orientation, ethnicity and socioeconomic background, those who are interested in the skateboarding should feel embraced by the skater community. The feeling of community begins with creating a space in the skate world where everyone feels welcome.


Research

The research conducted for this project is split into two sections one for the product and another for the user. For instance, the product research is composed of a competitive audit, which informed market gaps. While the user research’s interview based  quantitative data, informed the development of the initial user pain points, the personas with their respective problem statements, the user flow, and the journey map. Ultimately the goal of research is to empathize with users and define product problems.

Product

Since, there was no tangible product it made sense for the product research to be conducted through a competitive audit. For instance, the competitor analysis identified two obvious growth points, the first focused on a market gap and the second on inclusion. Regarding the market gap, there is a lack of online skate shops offering clients the option to build fully customizable boards. In reference to inclusion, all competitors analyzed were either devoid of or had insufficient diverse representation on their websites.

User

The user research is composed of general user demographics and user experience research. In order to gain general knowledge about the target audience, straightforward research on user demographics was conducted. Regarding the user experience research method, I found user interviews to be the most fitting for the user centered design approach I like to take.
‍
Findings from the user research are summarized as followed. For instance, the initial assumptions that primary users value diversity, view gender as highly nuanced, and are between the ages of 14 - 24 was confirmed by research on user demographic. While the user experience research revealed that users in this age group enjoy fast seamless online payment options, and a streamlined product searching process. Some insights gained after analyzing the research include fostering diversity through featured skaters, focusing on unique identities through customizable products that require minimal search effort to create, and offering various online payment options.

In summary, the user research informed the development of the personas, user pain points, the user flow, and the journey map.

User Flow
The design of the user flow is loosely based on observations of how users interacted with competitor apps, though it's tailored around the features that will be offered by the skate shop. In short, the user flow serves as an outline, early in the design process, with an intuitive path for the user to follow.

Journey Map
Due to the fact that the project has not yet been developed I opted for creating a future state user journey map, since it portrays the ideal user journey for a product that doesn't exist yet. It also provides a design direction with the help of information collected from the user interviews -- which were all collected by observing users interact with competitor apps. The future state journey map is a culmination of the initial planning of the design direction for the product -- there will be a lot more iterating, since the design phase is the next step in the process.

Ideation

Prior to starting the design process, it's ideal to generate ideas. The purpose of this step is not perfection, but rather a sort of warm up to wire-framing. In this phase I decided to begin with the crazy 8's sprint method followed by the creation of a site map.

Crazy 8's Design Sprint Method

For this project I decided to employ the Crazy 8's design sprint method, which consists of sketching 8 distinct ideas in the span of 8 minutes with the purpose of generating numerous creative ideas without the pressure of perfection looming over.

Design and Evaluation

In this section of the case study I'll be going over the steps I took to design the skate shop's responsive website. I'll explain my design process which begins with paper wireframes, a low risk brainstorming tool used to speed up the design workflow, I view them as an early information architecture planning aid. While the remaining steps are a cyclical iteration of perfecting digital wireframes and improving prototypes through the implementation of usability studies. Ultimately, the purpose of following through with the design process is to create a functional user friendly design. The end goal isn't perfection, but rather being aware that there is always room for improvement.

Usability Study

By following through with evaluating designs early on in the design process--as is the case with low fidelity prototypes--through testing I ensure that design issues are caught early on in the hopes of minimizing work later in the design process. From my perspective, the primary goals of early product testing is to discover and fix issues prior to investing too much time and resources.

This particular usability study, is unmoderated, composed of six participants and broken down into the following parts: a usability research study plan, the usability notes, an affinity diagram, the findings, and a brief explanation on the design iteration that resulted from the study.

Usability Research Study Plan

Creating a research plan prior to conducting usability research is a practice that I find extremely useful. Not only does it help me set research expectations, it also serves as an overview of the study that I can reference back to when needed. Overall, it provides a great starting point from which to begin thinking about the impact your usability research have.

Usability Study Notes
Taking notes of the usability study assists in keeping track of the users experience as they move through the website and provide a way to consolidate useful information that will be used to produce findings.
